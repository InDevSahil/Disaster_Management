Disaster Preparedness & Management

Being prepared can save lives. Learn how to prepare for disasters, what to do during emergencies, and how to recover afterward.

Create a comprehensive family emergency plan:

Discuss potential disasters that could affect your area and review family responsibilities.
Choose an out-of-area emergency contact person.
Plan escape routes from home, work, and school.
Designate a meeting place for your family to reunite.
Establish a communication plan including how you will contact each other.
Practice your plan regularly with family drills.
Post emergency numbers near phones and teach children how to call for help.
Review and update your plan annually or when family circumstances change.

üìã Preparedness Checklist

Before a Disaster

  • Make a family emergency plan
  • Prepare emergency kits
  • Secure your home (anchor heavy furniture, secure water heaters)
  • Know your community's warning systems
  • Stay informed about potential disasters in your area
  • Learn first aid and CPR
  • Check insurance coverage
  • Take photos of your property for insurance purposes

During a Disaster

  • Stay calm and follow your emergency plan
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Follow instructions from local authorities
  • If told to evacuate, do so immediately
  • If sheltering in place, stay indoors
  • Conserve food, water, and fuel
  • Help neighbors who may need assistance
  • Keep family members together

After a Disaster

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Check for damage to your home
  • Report damage to insurance company
  • Avoid driving unless necessary
  • Listen to local officials for updates
  • Help your neighbors, especially elderly or disabled
  • Document damage with photos
  • Seek mental health support if needed
